## 3. How we got there (the key decisions)

The copy is the product of a series of corrections that are worth preserving, because they define the voice:

- **Optimism, not anger.** The earlier About copy framed ICU as anti-jargon / fighting the industry. Uriah disowned it — not his voice, not true to his story. The whole spine was reframed around optimism (capability transfer + agility). *This angle must not be revived.*
- **Agility, not size, is the wedge.** "Being small is the advantage" risked excluding medium/larger clients. Evolved to **agility/adaptability** (size-agnostic), keeping small-business reassurance as objection-handling, never a ceiling.
- **"Bloat," not "red tape."** "Red tape" implied cutting corners. "Bloat" reads lean/efficient (positive), alliterates (Big Business Bloat), and echoes the ICU = Intensive Care Unit health metaphor.
- **Universal outcome language.** "Win the work" was too trades-specific; unified to "Get found / Get customers / Get time back" so it lands for retail/hospitality/creative too.
- **Pronoun discipline.** "I" = Uriah's work; "we" = partnership (client + Uriah) only; "ICU Media Design" = the entity. Never team-"we."
- **Honest proof, no fabrication.** No invented testimonials, no public price anchor. Word-of-mouth framing + real recent work + "deliberately small = full attention" carries §5 until real quotes land.
- **Wayfinding Home, ethos on About.** Referral traffic arrives pre-trusted, so Home routes and inspires; "why me" is demoted and the full story is saved for About.
