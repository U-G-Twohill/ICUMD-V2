## What "Done" Means

A v1 launch passes the business-card test if all of the following are true:

1. The site is live at `https://icumediadesign.co.nz` on Hostinger static hosting.
2. Every page loads cleanly on desktop and mobile, no broken images, no fictional content.
3. The contact form sends real mail to `hello@icumediadesign.co.nz` and Uriah receives it in his fresh business Gmail.
4. The portfolio page shows three real projects (updated InfoFocus 2026, updated Simmons Trucking 2026, current CarLovers).
5. Nothing on the site claims something that isn't true (no fake testimonials, no fake industries, no phone number, no in-house team, no NZ hosting capability claims).
6. Uriah would happily put the URL on a business card and hand it to a prospect.

Anything beyond that is out of scope for v1.

## Risks and Watch-Outs

- **Domain renewal lapse** — if `.co.nz` renewal slips, deploy is gated. Treat as a launch-prep gate that must pass.
- **Zoho Free tier limits** — single user, 5GB inbox, web/IMAP access. Fine for solo. If clients start expecting calendar invites with branded sender, revisit Workspace.
- **Counter regression** — the audit flagged the counter "0 → target" animation can show wrong values if not yet fired. Handled by SF11 deferral but watch on launch-prep smoke test.
- **Shader on low-power devices** — pause-when-offscreen + reduced-motion respect in `shader-bg.js` mitigates, but smoke test on a low-end phone before launch.
- **CarLovers as portfolio entry** — if its current state doesn't represent Uriah's current quality bar, the launch portfolio drops to 2 strong + 1 weak. Reassess at launch-prep. Also: at launch-prep we'll need CarLovers' live URL, a screenshot/banner, and a 2-3 sentence project description — gather these in advance.
- **Chunk 9 stalls without pricing decisions** — GST treatment and ecommerce starting price must be decided by Uriah before Chunk 9 runs. If Uriah hasn't answered, skip Chunk 9 and circle back; don't guess. Other chunks aren't blocked.
- **NZ English false positives** — blanket find-and-replace on NZ English could touch CSS class names (`text-center`, `flex-center`), property values (`color:`, `text-align: center`), or JSON keys. Chunk 10 must be a manual visible-text review, not automated replace.
- **Pre-flight commit window** — between Chunk 0 (commit dormant work) and Chunk 1 (first edits), the working tree is clean. If anything goes wrong mid-Chunk-1, `git reset --hard HEAD` rolls back to the pre-flight checkpoint without losing dormant work.
